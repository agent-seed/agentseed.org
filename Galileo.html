Galileo Galilei

Italian philosopher, astronomer and mathematician

Galileo Galilei was an Italian astronomer, physicist and engineer. He was born in 1564 in Pisa Italy, he had a relatively normal childhood (for the 1500's that is). Early in life he made many breakthroughs that are fundamental to modern science (such as his studies of gravity, speed, propulsion, the principle of relativity, among others), He also invented many groundbreaking machines, the modern compass being a notable one. Although he invented many useful things, he spent most of his time gazing at the stars, and theorizing about mathematical formulas. He made great improvements in the field of physics, discovering that objects of different mass fall at the same speed. Galileo also was a musician, and made interesting discoveries about the relationship between pitch and tension. Another groundbreaking invention of his is the mirrored telescope. Early in life he learned to grind telescope lenses at the local glasses shop, which led him to learn how mirrorless telescopes work (without any outside help). The life of Galileo was full of many great discoveries, but perhaps most important is his work on gravity (one could say it's riveting), it revolutionized the common understanding of the world, this was a concept that was later developed by Newton, and expanded into defining the way objects interact in space.
